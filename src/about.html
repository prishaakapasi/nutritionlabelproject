About

The COMMUNITY RECIPES tool is meant to help online communities craft intentional digital spaces that can learn from, connect to, and coordinate with a broader network of communities. As part of a collaboration between Princeton HCI and Bonfire Networks, it's also part of a research program on how to move toward a community-owned and -governed internet. Here's what we're working on:

  • Iterating on the RECIPE maker through interviews and surveys
  • Collating a dataset of RECIPES from real-life communities into an open library
  • Co-designing mechanisms for communities to build "trust bubbles"

A chunk of this work is informed and driven by design workshops we ran in Summer 2025, findings from which are summarized in this academic pre-print. You can reach out to the current contributor team below to learn more.
